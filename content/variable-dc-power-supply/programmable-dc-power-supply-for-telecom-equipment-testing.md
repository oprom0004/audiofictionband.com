## Technical Requirements for Modern Telecom Testing

Testing telecom hardware requires rigorous adherence to power quality standards. When selecting a power supply for these applications, several key electrical parameters must be prioritized to ensure accurate data acquisition and equipment safety.

### Precision and Stability
Telecom equipment often operates within narrow voltage tolerances. The ETM series, including models ranging from the ETM-8001 (1A) to the ETM-8006 (6A), provides the high-resolution control necessary to simulate precise operating environments. Maintaining accuracy in voltage and current regulation is paramount, and all AFB Power units are calibrated conforming to [NIST Electrical Measurement Guidelines](https://www.nist.gov/pml/div683/about/calibration) to ensure traceability and measurement integrity.

### Ripple Noise and Transient Response
High-frequency switching in telecom components is sensitive to power supply noise. Low ripple noise is essential to prevent false failures during EMI/EMC compliance testing. Furthermore, the transient response time of the power supply determines how quickly it can recover from load steps—a critical factor when testing power-hungry cellular infrastructure that fluctuates rapidly during peak traffic cycles. The ETM series is optimized for fast response times, ensuring that voltage sag or overshoot remains within acceptable thresholds during dynamic load changes.

## Engineering Advantages of the ETM Series

The AFB Power ETM series is built to handle the high-voltage demands of modern telecommunications, offering up to 800V DC output. This capability is vital for testing high-voltage DC distribution systems and battery backup units (BBUs) commonly found in central offices and remote cell sites.

### CC/CV Crossover and Remote Sensing
The transition between Constant Current (CC) and Constant Voltage (CV) modes must be seamless to prevent damaging sensitive telecom circuitry during startup or fault conditions. The ETM series features an advanced CC/CV crossover architecture that prevents voltage spikes during mode switching. Additionally, remote sense capabilities allow engineers to compensate for voltage drops across long test leads, ensuring that the voltage measured at the device under test (DUT) is exactly what the programmable interface dictates.

### Voltage Sag Immunity and SEMI F47 Compliance
Telecom equipment is often subjected to unstable utility power. To ensure mission-critical uptime, devices must be tested for voltage sag immunity. By utilizing the programmable features of the ETM-8001 through ETM-8006, engineers can program custom voltage sag profiles, verifying that the DUT meets performance standards similar to the SEMI F47 specification. This proactive testing approach identifies potential power-stage vulnerabilities before hardware is deployed in the field.
