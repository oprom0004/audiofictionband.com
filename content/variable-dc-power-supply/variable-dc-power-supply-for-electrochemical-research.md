Electrochemical research—ranging from electroplating and anodization to advanced battery cycling and hydrogen electrolysis—demands power delivery systems that transcend basic benchtop functionality. For high-voltage electrochemical setups, the power supply must maintain absolute control over the current-voltage (I-V) characteristics to prevent unwanted side reactions or thermal runaway. ## Technical Criteria for Electrochemical Instrumentation

When selecting a variable DC power supply for electrochemical research, the engineer must evaluate the supply's ability to handle highly reactive or capacitive loads. 

### CC/CV Crossover and Transient Response
The transition between constant current (CC) and constant voltage (CV) modes is critical. In electrochemical cells, the impedance changes dynamically as the reaction progresses. An inferior power supply may exhibit significant overshoot during this crossover, potentially damaging sensitive electrodes or corrupting data. The AFB Power ETM series, including models like the ETM-8001 (1A) through the ETM-8006 (6A), features high-speed CC/CV crossover circuitry designed to mitigate these spikes, ensuring a seamless transition that protects the integrity of the electrochemical interface.

### Ripple Noise and Stability
Electrochemical measurements are inherently sensitive to noise. High-frequency ripple superimposed on the DC output can induce measurement errors in potentiostatic or galvanostatic experiments. Our ETM series provides ultra-low ripple noise, ensuring that the DC signal remains clean enough for high-precision electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) and other diagnostic techniques where signal-to-noise ratios are paramount.
